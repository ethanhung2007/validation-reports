Analysis

Two-board cross-validation is the strongest evidence. The same 13 shapes were measured on both the Aetina Alpha (pre-production) and the production Metis Card. Card is systematically slightly lower than Alpha (12/13 points), with larger differences at small N converging at large N — a small cross-SDK-version shift within tolerance. The freeze was lifted and the Alpha fit retained because the Card confirms the model structure, not just a point estimate.

Prefill and decode regimes bridge correctly. The M=1 decode anchor (41.83 µs, 200.5 GOP/s) lies on the leftward extrapolation of the prefill affine line. This is a non-trivial consistency check: the two models were fit independently (G_eff from throughput measurements, affine from tile latency sweep), yet they agree at the M=1 boundary to within measurement noise.

Residency cliff is the key Phase 1.5 correction. The old tile-sum model underestimated FFN/lm_head latency by ~3× because it applied the SRAM-resident G_eff formula beyond the knee. The cliff model (knee ≈ 8.2M params, spill floor 70 GOP/s) corrects this. Hold-out median 3.3% vs old model median 31% is a ~10× improvement. Real decode FFN and lm_head GEMVs (K·N ≥ 16M) fall in the spill regime, making this correction critical for end-to-end latency accuracy.

SRAM-tiling wall was underestimated by ~2×. The old assumption of M_MAX=256 is incorrect; the real wall is at M≈510 (M=508 succeeds, M=511 fails). This roughly doubles the effective prefill batch size before hitting the compilation limit. The wall is real and SRAM-related; only its position was wrong.

Compute ceiling is not modelled — intentionally. Gmax = 333.67 GOP/s is only 0.156% of the 214 TOPS datasheet ceiling. The decode kernel is so memory-bound (all 27 roofline checks show BW-limited, not compute-limited) that the compute ceiling never constrains latency. Modelling it would add complexity without changing predictions. This is documented as Issue #16 in the Phase 1 readiness table.

Known gaps for Phase 2. Three items remain extrapolated rather than validated: prefill M > 508 (beyond compile wall), multi-tile K·N > 17M (beyond native compile envelope, floor extrapolated), and edge topology noc_efficiency=0.9 (pending edge silicon calibration). None block Phase 1 use; all are flagged in the readiness table.
